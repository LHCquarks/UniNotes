Jordan's Lemma states that if
- $f$ is a continuous function in $\{z\in\mathbb C: \text{Im}(z)\ge 0, |z|\ge S\}$ for some positive number $S$
- $\Gamma_R$ is the upper half of the circle with center 0 and radius $R$
- $|f(z)| \le M_R$ for all $z \in \Gamma_R$ where $\lim_{R\rightarrow \infty} M_R = 0$
The for any $\xi > 0$,
$$
\begin{align}
\lim_{R \rightarrow \infty} \left|\int_{\Gamma_R} e^{i\xi z} f(z)dz\right| = 0
\end{align}
$$
