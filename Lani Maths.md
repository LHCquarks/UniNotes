## Work
$$
\begin{align}
W = F\cdot s
\end{align}
$$
where:
- $W$ is the work done
- $F$ is the force applied
- $s$ is the displacement (distance from the start to the end)

### Example
$1$ tonne Car initially moving at $60kmh^{-1}$ experiences $5N$ of force from friction over $1km$. What is it's final velocity?
#### Convert $60 kmh^{-1}$ into $ms^{-1}$
$$
\begin{align}
60kmh^{-1} &= \frac{60 km}{h}\\
&= \frac{60 km}{h} \frac{h}{60 m}\\ 
&= \frac{1km}{m} \frac{m}{60 s}\\ 
&= \frac{1km}{60s} \frac{1000m}{km}\\ 
&= \frac{100m}{6s} \\ 
&= 16.\bar{6}\frac{m}{s} \\ 
\end{align}
$$
#### Find initial energy of the car
$$
\begin{align}
KE &= \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \\
&= \frac{1}{2} 1000 \times {16.\bar{6}}^2 \\
&= 500 \times {16.\bar{6}}^2 \\
&= 138888.7778 J
\end{align}
$$
#### Find work done
$$
\begin{align}
W &= F\times s \\
&= 5 \times 1000 \\
&= 5000 J
\end{align}
$$
#### Find final energy
$$
\begin{align}
E_f &= E_i - W \\
&= 138888.7778  - 5000 \\
&= 133888J
\end{align}
$$
#### Find velocity
$$
\begin{align}
KE &= \frac{1}{2}mv^2\\
133888 &= \frac{1}{2} 1000 v^2 \\
2 \times 133888 &= 1000 v^2 \\
277777.5556 &= 1000 v^2 \\
277.7775556 &= v^2 \\
277.7775556 &= v^2 \\
\end{align}
$$
